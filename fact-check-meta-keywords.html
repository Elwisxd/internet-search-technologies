Claim:

Adding many <meta name="keywords"> tags improves your Google search ranking.

Verdict: ‚ùå False

Google has officially stated that it does not use the meta keywords tag in its ranking algorithms. This has been the case since at least 2009.

Evidence:

  • Google Search Central Blog (2009): Google does not use the keywords meta tag
  • Confirmed by Google spokespersons including John Mueller on multiple occasions.

Explanation:

The meta keywords tag was widely abused for keyword stuffing in the early 2000s. Since then, search engines have moved towards smarter algorithms that prioritize quality, relevance, and intent.

As shown in the example above, meta keyword tags may still exist on websites, but they are ignored by Google.
